Activity checks

Check the identity has existed over time

Checking the identity has existed over time will lower the risk of you accepting either:

  • a synthetic identity
  • an identity that belongs to someone who's died

You can check this by looking for interactions the person has had with other organisations. Some examples of these interactions are:

  • credit card transactions
  • student loan repayments
  • mortgage payments
  • gas or electricity account payments
  • when someone signs in to an online bank or retail account

You can also check if any records exist that show if the person has been known by other organisations over time, such as their:

  • health records
  • employment records
  • school records

The highest score you can get for this check is 4.
