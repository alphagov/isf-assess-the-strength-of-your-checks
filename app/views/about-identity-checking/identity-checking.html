How identity checking works

Identity checking is made up of a combination of 5 pieces; evidence, validity, activity, fraud and verification.

1. Evidence

Some pieces of evidence are 'stronger' than others, which means they will be harder to:

  • forge (when an existing piece of evidence is changed to make it look like it belongs to someone else)
  • counterfeit (when a piece of evidence is created from scratch)

2. Validity

Check the evidence is genuine or valid. This gives you protection against people using evidence that is lost, stolen, expired, revoked or synthetic.

3. Activity

Check the identity exists in the real world. This gives you protection against people who have created synthetic identities.

4. Fraud

Understand if they are at high risk of fraud. This gives you protection against people using evidence that has been reported stolen, or impersonating someone who has died or is high profile.

5. Verification

Make sure the person is the same person as on their evidence. This gives you protection against people pretending to be someone else.

Having confidence in an identity

Doing different parts of the identity checking process will help you build up confidence in an identity so that you can be sure someone is who they say they are.
